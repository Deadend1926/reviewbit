10 Best Low Stress Jobs That Pay Well (Even Without A College Degree)

People seek low-stress jobs that offer a balance between financial stability and mental well-being due to burnout, a desire for a career change, or the pursuit of remote work opportunities. Minimal pressure, manageable workloads, and a healthy work-life balance characterize these roles.
We’ll provide a comprehensive list of 10 low-stress jobs that pay well, without a degree, for a healthy work-life balance.

1. Microworker

Microwork involves completing small, simple tasks online, such as data labeling, surveys, or app testing. Microtasking requires minimal skills and offers complete flexibility, allowing you to work at your own pace without the pressure of deadlines or supervisors. 
Platforms like ReviewBit provide opportunities to earn money through various microtasks, including games and surveys. You can earn money without investment, have fun while watching videos, or polish your skills with app testing. 
Explore such low-stress jobs that pay well without a degree, and become a microtasker. It’ll allow you to set your schedule and supplement your income. It’s a fully remote job, so you can work at your own pace from the comfort of your home.

2. Virtual assistant

Virtual assistants handle administrative tasks for various clients. Such a job typically involves scheduling, email management, and data analysis and entry. This role offers flexible hours and the possibility of remote jobs, making it ideal for those seeking a low-stress environment. 
It’s a high-paying job with an average of $28/hour in the US. Becoming a virtual assistant can improve your mental and physical health as you can set your hours and prioritize health checkups, as you’ll work remotely.
Beginners can start without a college degree and gradually increase their income by acquiring more clients. However, such financial stability can come with tight deadlines, so be aware of your stress tolerance. 
If you’re interested in becoming a virtual assistant, check out job postings on freelance websites like Upwork or Fiverr. Focus on building a strong portfolio and scale your own business with additional services for clients.

3. Data entry clerk

Data entry clerks input information into systems, a repetitive and task-oriented role that suits individuals who prefer minimal interaction and the structured nature of work. Attention to detail and the ability to use data entry tools (e.g., Excel) are crucial. However, it’s one of the many low-stress jobs that pay well without a degree or formal education.
With a strong skill set, data entry can be an ideal job for students since it’s mostly a low-stress, remote job, and you can set your hours. It’s also a great part-time job option, allowing you to work flexible hours while managing school or other commitments. You can expect to earn about $40,000 annually in the US as an entry specialist.
Check freelance websites like Upwork or Fiverr for legitimate opportunities. Also, chat with potential clients and figure out their expectations – some companies confuse data analysis with data entry and require a bachelor’s degree. It’s crucial to talk over these details before starting your gig.

4. Freelance transcriptionist

Freelance transcriptionists convert audio files into written text. You’ll work independently, so this role suits introverts and people who prefer less socialization and a low-stress environment. You can start it as a side gig while you’re on the job search and see if it fits your expectations and career goals.
Many jobs don’t require a bachelor’s degree or tight deadlines. Instead, they offer flexibility and opportunity to specialize in high-paying fields like medical or legal. Such specialization will help you generate more money and maintain a long-term partnership with your customers as you’ll be considered an expert in these fields.
Essential tools include noise-canceling headphones and transcription software. Don’t invest in expensive gear at the beginning and see if it’s something you like. To get started, platforms such as Rev and TranscribeMe offer opportunities for beginners to get started.

5. Librarian technician or assistant

Working in a library provides a calm and structured environment. You don’t need to be a librarian, as these roles often require formal education. Instead, library technicians and assistants handle administrative tasks like cataloging, shelving, and assisting patrons.
You can enjoy low-stress levels by checking open positions on your local library's website or asking around for opportunities. Craft a well-structured resume and highlight your organizational skills, ability to maintain clean documentation, and passion for literature.
Additionally, you can look into certification programs if you’re interested in working with books. However, a high school diploma will suffice for entry-level or administrative roles. 

6. Delivery driver (non-urgent)

Non-urgent delivery jobs, such as grocery, pharmacy, or package delivery, offer independence and the opportunity to listen to music or podcasts while on the job. You can enjoy low stress levels and take pleasure in driving local routes.
However, it won’t be a high-paying gig. For example, non-urgent medical drivers earn $12 per hour in the US. Nevertheless, you can enjoy the peaceful environment, no tight deadlines, and the ability to take part in patient care. Check your driver’s license before registering – you’ll need to have a valid card and be of age to drive.
These roles typically don't require a college degree, just a clean driving record. Research the companies or apps offering these gigs and check their reviews. It’ll help you avoid scams. You can use well-known job posting boards like Indeed, but use the keyword “non-urgent” in your job search.

7. Groundskeeper or gardener

If you’re looking for a low-stress job, many people suggest working as a groundskeeper or gardener. They maintain outdoor spaces, offering low-pressure routine work and the benefit of physical movement and time in nature, which can boost mental health. 
These roles often offer seasonal demand and flexibility, either as freelance or employed positions. Make sure to maintain your client base and stress levels during these times, so it doesn’t get overwhelming.
It’s a high-paying job where you can earn up to $18 per hour and charge your clients for extra services like watering, mowing, or redecorating. Sometimes, it involves working outside your role, and gardeners can work as floral designers or choose related career goals.
If you want to get started, ask around your neighborhood for opportunities. Additionally, use social media or bulletin boards to offer your services locally. Always check job postings in local media outlets or online.

8. Graphic design freelancer

Graphic designers can enjoy a low-stress environment if they maintain a constant client base. You’ll need professional skills and equipment while creating art as a freelancer. Adobe, Canva, and other tools will be crucial to your job.
Also, it can be a side job to generate additional income if you’re working as a designer somewhere else. However, focus on managing your workload and avoiding stress.
Platforms like Fiverr and Upwork offer opportunities for beginner graphic designers with businesses posting gigs daily. Make sure to check it continuously to build relationships and reduce stress associated with financial risks while working as a freelancer.
Also, set clear boundaries and manage workload effectively. Don’t overpromise to businesses and pick realistic deadlines. Maintain a healthy work-life balance to avoid burnout that other graphic designers can feel. Otherwise, freelance design can be a side hustle with low stress and high enjoyment.

9. Pet sitter or dog walker

Working with animals can be peaceful and rewarding. Pet sitting and dog walking offer flexibility and a low entry barrier. Your duties can include walking the pets, feeding them, and taking care of them while the owners are away for an extra charge.
Building trust with clients is key to increasing earning potential through multiple bookings. Make sure to account for any allergies or behaviours of the pets to give them the best care. Create a peaceful environment for yourself and the pets so that you can maintain a constant client base.
Platforms like Rover host a variety of job postings. Filter your area and check if anyone is looking for a pet sitter locally. Additionally, you can start small and negotiate salary once you build trust.
It’s a high-paying role offering up to $20 per hour in the US. You’ll get paid to walk pets, but you can charge extra for grooming or feeding, so your salary can double. However, consider liability insurance or bonding for professionalism for any unforeseen circumstances, and lower stress.

10. Remote customer support (chat-based)

Text or chat-based customer support roles are more structured and manageable than traditional call center jobs. These positions often offer scheduling flexibility and training provided by employers as they’re entry-level roles.
Businesses hire college students or people looking for flexible schedules to provide customer support around the clock. If you wish, you can choose to work day, evening, or night shifts. 
Expect to earn up to $20 per hour in the US as a remote customer support representative. Such roles tend to have more stress, so choose remote and chat-based jobs to manage it. Check websites like WeWorkRemotely for such opportunities. You can find openings for jobs at Google, Amazon, or GitHub.
Also, strong communication skills matter more than a bachelor’s or associate's degree for this job. Make sure you’re polite and clear in your messages to provide the best customer support you can.
