Get Paid To Listen To Music: 8 Legit Platforms For Music Fans

Monika Ivanauskaite
Updated on December 5, 2025
Share:
Want to learn how to get paid to listen to music? There are platforms that actually reward music fans to review music, leave feedback, or curate playlists. This is a great option for stay-at-home moms, students, and even high schoolers who want to earn some extra cash in their free time. 
This guide breaks down eight legit websites where you can earn just by doing what you already love – listening to music. Let’s dive in!

1. Slice the Pie

Slice the Pie is one of the most popular platforms where you can make money listening to music. You earn by reviewing songs from artists, rating tracks, and leaving short feedback. Unlike other platforms, Slice the Pie partners with record labels and independent artists to gather fans’ thoughts before official releases.
  • Payments are made via a linked PayPal account once you reach the $10 minimum.
  • Reviews are rated – well-written reviews with specific opinions earn more.
  • You’ll usually listen to 90 seconds before submitting your thoughts.
Pros:
  • Easy sign-up with no prior experience.
  • Great for music lovers who want to earn money in their own time.
  • Opportunity to listen to and review music from a wide range of genres.
Cons:
  • Low payouts per review (typically cents).
  • It takes time to build up enough reviews for consistent earnings.
It’s a simple way to write reviews for money while discovering musicians across fresh genres. Even though you won’t get paid a lot per track, it’s still a fun and accessible way to explore the music industry.

2. Current Rewards (formerly Current.us)

Current Rewards is a free app that lets you make money from your phone by listening to music. It runs in the background as you stream songs through built-in internet radio channels. While it doesn’t integrate directly with Spotify, the app offers hundreds of genre-based stations to keep you entertained.
You’ll earn points for every minute of music playing, plus extra points for completing simple tasks like surveys or checking out offers. Once you collect enough points, you can redeem them for PayPal account payouts, gift cards, or even gadgets.
Pros:
  • Easy to use on the go
  • Works passively in the background
  • Offers extra earning options beyond listening to music
Cons:
  • Slow accumulation of points
  • Doesn’t support Spotify playlist streaming
It’s a decent choice for music lovers who want a low-effort way to earn extra cash while multitasking or relaxing.

3. Playlist Push

If you’ve built a solid Spotify playlist, Playlist Push lets you get paid to listen to music on Spotify and share your honest thoughts. This platform connects artists with popular playlist curators, helping them promote their new music through quality reviews.
To qualify, you’ll need at least 1,000 followers and must pass a quick approval process that checks the quality and activity of your playlists. Once accepted, you’ll receive songs tailored to your audience and get paid per review you post.
Tips to grow your playlist:
  • Stick to playlist genres that are trending
  • Share your playlists across social media
  • Keep songs fresh by adding new music regularly
This is a great option to earn money from something you already enjoy, especially if you’re looking to earn when bored or make the most of your free time.

4. HitPredictor

HitPredictor lets music fans rate upcoming songs before they hit streaming platforms. It’s a fun way to listen to new tracks and share your opinion while earning points. These points can be exchanged for Amazon gift cards or used for sweepstakes entries. You’ll be part of the early feedback loop that helps shape what gets released to the public.
While you won’t get paid to listen to music in cash directly, it’s still a rewarding hobby for curious listeners who love discovering fresh genres. The platform is open worldwide and doesn’t require building large playlists or having a social following.
Pros:
  • Easy to join with no approval process
  • Discover unreleased music first
  • Redeem points for gift cards
Cons:
  • Points accumulate slowly
  • Not a consistent way to earn money
It's a low-pressure way to enter the music industry for those who want to enjoy playing music while earning a few perks.

5. RadioEarn

RadioEarn is a passive way to get paid to listen to music by streaming internet radio. Once set up, it runs quietly in your browser or desktop app while you go about your day. As you stream, you earn points based on your listening time, with no need to choose specific songs or leave a review.
How to begin:
  • Create a free account
  • Choose a radio stream
  • Let it play in the background
Points convert into money through PayPal, Bitcoin, or Amazon gift cards once you hit the payout threshold.
It’s a smart choice for music enthusiasts with a stable internet connection who don’t want to write music reviews or manage playlists. Just hit play and let the platform track your uptime automatically.
Although the earnings are slow, it's one of the easiest ways to earn paid passive income. Most people leave it running while working, studying, or relaxing at home.

6. Music Xray

Music Xray connects music enthusiasts with independent artists looking for listeners' thoughts. Once you sign up, you’ll receive email alerts when new songs match your preferences. Every time you listen to music and provide feedback, you’ll earn at least $0.10 per track, making it a simple way to earn a bit while enjoying tunes.
The process is simple:
  • Create a profile and list your favorite genres
  • Get matched with new music
  • Listen, then leave your thoughts or a quick review
Payments are made via PayPal account once you reach the minimum withdrawal amount. It's a great way to get paid to listen, but don’t expect a high volume of daily songs – tracks are limited and based on your profile.
Still, for those who enjoy discovering new artists, exploring different genres, and providing honest opinion, it’s a fun, low-effort way to earn extra money without pressure or strict deadlines.

7. Cash4Minutes

Cash4Minutes lets you get paid to listen to music using unused mobile minutes. Instead of streaming from your browser, you dial in and stay on the call while music or radio plays. It's fully automated – no interaction required during the session, and it works seamlessly on both smartphones and traditional mobile devices.
To start:
  • Register your number
  • Choose your preferred music streaming services or stations
  • Earn with each minute you stay connected
It works globally and is ideal for listeners with unlimited talk plans. Payments are sent via PayPal account or cryptocurrency.
Pros:
  • Totally hands-free
  • Runs in the background
  • Works on any phone
Cons:
  • Paid minutes are limited daily
  • You won’t earn much without consistency
It's a clever option for those wanting to make money passively while doing other things. Pair it with other platforms to maximize your daily income and streamline your side hustles.

8. JumpTask 

JumpTask is a flexible platform worth exploring if you enjoy earning money online. While it doesn’t specialize in music gigs, it occasionally features simple tasks related to audio or providing feedback, though these are rare and not guaranteed.
The real benefit lies in its variety. You can still earn by doing microtasking, such as:
  • Taking surveys
  • Testing new apps
  • Playing games
  • Watching content or doing light research
Everything is available through the JumpTask dashboard. It’s designed for people who want to earn money on their time and prefer remote-friendly tasks with little effort.
Even if you're here to get paid to listen, JumpTask offers a smart way to diversify your income. If you’re curious how it works, you can explore microtasks or dive deeper into how microtasking fits into your day.
Combining platforms like this helps you earn more consistently, even when no new music is playing.
