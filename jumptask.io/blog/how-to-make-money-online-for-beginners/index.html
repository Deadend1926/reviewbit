Complete online surveys and tasks on reward platforms

If you’re looking to earn a little extra money online without committing to a full side hustle, get-paid-to (GPT) platforms offer a low-effort option where you’re paid small amounts for short activities.
These activities, also known as microtasks, can include anything from completing surveys and watching short videos to liking posts on social media and even playing games.
The good thing about this money-making method is that you don’t need any previous knowledge or special skills. What you will need is a stable internet connection and a few free hours a day to go through the tasks.
One of the most popular GPT platforms with global access is JumpTask. This platform allows you to earn money online without an investment by completing simple tasks from anywhere.
You can earn money online with JumpTask by: 
  • Answering surveys: You answer quick questions about your habits or opinions. Companies use the answers for research, and you get paid a small amount for your time.
  • Watching short videos or ads: You’ll sometimes be asked to watch quick promo videos or ads and let them play all the way through. Once the timer runs out, you earn a small reward.
  • Clicking links and visiting websites: Some tasks simply ask you to click a link or open a website and stay on it for a few seconds. These are quick and easy, especially when you’re short on time.
  • Trying apps or games: You might be asked to install an app or try a game for a short time, sometimes with a goal like opening it once or reaching a specific level.
  • Social media tasks: These can include joining a community, following an account, or interacting with content on platforms like social media or messaging apps.
Many people start earning within the first few hours and typically make around $2 to $10 per day, depending on the time spent. 

Sell unwanted items or resell for profit

Selling items you already own is one of the simplest ways to make money online as a beginner. 
To get started, choose one of the following platforms based on what you want to sell:
  • Facebook Marketplace: Best for local sales and quick pickups without shipping or platform fees.
  • eBay: Best for reaching buyers nationwide, with the option to sell internationally.
  • Vinted or Depop: Best for selling clothing, shoes, and accessories.
To sell items faster, try the following:
  • Focus on items with consistent market demand, such as clothes in good condition, small electronics, books, or household items.
  • Take clear photos in good lighting so buyers can see exactly what they are getting.
  • Write honest descriptions that clearly explain the item’s condition and size.
Once you feel comfortable selling items you already own, you can move into reselling for profit by buying low-priced items and listing them at a higher price online.
This can be a great way to make money as a woman or to make money as a guy, as you can work on your own schedule, start with very little risk, and scale at your own pace while building practical skills like pricing, customer communication, and inventory management at the same time.

Participate in user testing or market research panels

Businesses pay for user testing to understand how real people interact with their websites or apps, and you earn money by participating in those tests. 
You use a site or app as instructed, complete simple tasks like finding information or signing up, and explain what feels clear or confusing as you go. Companies would then review this feedback to improve design, navigation, and overall user experience.
Bear in mind that these tests can be competitive and may not appear every day. Another limitation is that some tests require you to qualify based on your profile, location, or device.
Popular user testing and market research platforms include:
  • UserTesting: Best for beginners who want short tests with clear instructions and straightforward payouts.
  • Respondent: Best for users who have specific work or industry experience and want access to higher-paying research studies.
  • Trymata: Best for quick website and app tests that require basic feedback and minimal setup.
A typical test can last 10 to 30 minutes. You need a quiet space, a microphone, and clear communication skills, but no previous experience.

Beginner-friendly freelance jobs to try from home

If you want more control over your income, the following freelance jobs offer beginner-friendly ways to earn online by completing simple tasks for clients.

Offer virtual assistance or data entry

Becoming a virtual assistant involves helping individuals or small businesses with basic online tasks they do not have time or the means to handle themselves. 
As a beginner, you might be in charge of:
  • managing emails
  • scheduling appointments
  • organizing digital files
  • updating spreadsheets
  • posting simple content online
If you want something even more straightforward, data entry is a good option. In this role, you would focus on entering information into systems or spreadsheets. 
The learning curve is low, the work is repetitive, and it can suit you well if you want no-frills work that pays for consistency rather than creativity.
Beginner-friendly platforms where you can find these gigs include:
  • Upwork: Best for beginners who want access to a wide range of entry-level freelance jobs.
  • Freelancer: Best for simple task-based projects that allow you to bid on short-term or ongoing work.
  • Fiverr: Best for offering basic services at fixed prices, even if you are just starting and building experience.

Try writing or graphic design as a side gig

As a beginner writer, you might be responsible for content creation or basic digital marketing tasks like:
  • writing blog posts
  • product descriptions
  • social media captions
  • website copy
If you prefer visual work, you can explore side gigs for graphic designers, such as 
  • creating social media graphics
  • basic flyers
  • presentation slides
  • simple logos 
Many of these tasks help businesses build a stronger social media presence, which is why beginners who can create clear and consistent visuals are always in demand.
To get your first clients, try doing the following steps:
  1. Start with small projects that match your current skill level rather than aiming for high-paying work immediately.
  2. Create a few sample pieces, even if they are mock projects, to show what you can do.
  3. Apply consistently and respond to client messages quickly to build trust early on.
Beginner-friendly platforms to find writing or design gigs include:
  • Fiverr: Best for beginners who want to offer simple services at fixed prices and build experience gradually.
  • Upwork: Best for finding short-term or ongoing writing and design projects with clear requirements.
  • Freelancer: Best for bidding on small creative tasks that help you gain reviews and confidence.
Here are a couple of tools you can use to get started:
  • Google Docs: Best for writing, editing, and sharing content with clients easily.
  • Canva: Best for beginners creating simple graphics without design experience.
  • Grammarly: Helpful for checking grammar and clarity in writing tasks.

Use your language skills for online tutoring or transcription

If you speak English fluently or understand spoken language well, you can turn those skills into income.
You can engage in online tutoring to help people practice English through conversation, basic lessons, or guided exercises. You do not need to be a certified teacher, as many platforms accept non-native speakers. 
Transcription is another beginner-friendly option. In this role, you would listen to audio or video recordings and type out what you hear. The work pays based on accuracy and speed, not creativity. If you can understand different accents and type fast, this can be a steady way to earn extra cash.
Both tutoring and transcription require very little setup. You only need a laptop, headphones, a stable internet connection, and decent communication skills.
Some of the most beginner-friendly platforms to explore language tutoring and transcription:
  • Cambly: Best for conversational English tutoring with flexible scheduling and no teaching credentials required.
  • Rev: Best for beginners who want straightforward transcription work with clear guidelines and task-based pay.
  • TranscribeMe: Best for short transcription tasks that help you build experience gradually.

Long-term online income paths worth exploring

Once you feel comfortable earning online, you can explore the options below to grow into steady income sources you can control long-term.

Start a blog or niche website

To run a blog or niche website, you would need to create helpful articles around a particular subject and attract readers through search engines or social media.
As a beginner, you do not need to write about everything. It works best when you focus on one clear topic, such as budgeting, fitness, parenting, travel, or beginner guides. 
With blogs, you can make money in three main ways:
  1. Ads, where you earn money based on the number of views your content receives.
  2. Affiliate links, where you recommend products and earn a commission when someone makes a purchase.
  3. Digital products, such as guides, templates, or checklists that you create and sell yourself.
You should expect this to take time, as most blogs do not make money in the first few weeks or even months, but once you build traffic, the income can become consistent and scalable.
Here are some of the most beginner-friendly tools that can come in handy:
  • WordPress: Best for building a professional website, with thousands of free themes and plugins and no coding required.
  • Affordable hosting providers like Bluehost or Hostinger: Best for getting your blog online with low monthly costs and a simple setup.
  • Google Docs: Best for writing, editing, and organizing blog posts before publishing them on your site.
  • Free SEO tools like Google Search Console and Ubersuggest: Helpful for finding topics people already search for and understanding how your content can rank.

Grow a YouTube or short video channel

If you are comfortable speaking or showing simple processes in front of the camera, creating videos on a YouTube channel or other platforms can be a great fit for you. 
Please note that you do not need expensive equipment or editing skills to start, as many beginners use just a smartphone and natural lighting.
You can make money from videos in three simple ways:
  1. Ads, where platforms pay you based on the number of views your videos receive.
  2. Affiliate links, where you earn a commission when viewers buy products you mention.
  3. Brand promotions, where companies pay you to feature or mention their products in your videos.
You can explore some of the most popular beginner-friendly video ideas, such as product reviews, simple tutorials, or how-to guides. 

Sell digital products or offer online services

Selling digital products works much like a passive income; you create something once and sell it over and over again. 
You can start with some of the following digital products:
  • Planners for daily, weekly, or monthly organization
  • Checklists for routines, goals, or tasks
  • Templates for CVs, budgeting, scheduling, or planning
You can also offer simple online services based on what you already know, such as:
  • Resume formatting or basic editing
  • Simple social media management
  • Email inbox organization
  • Online courses on topics you are familiar with
Some of the best platforms where you can sell products or services are:
  • Etsy: An online store, best for selling digital downloads like planners, templates, and trackers to a large built-in audience.
  • Gumroad: Best for selling digital files directly with a simple setup and no storefront management.
  • Payhip: Best for beginners who want to sell digital products with built-in payment and delivery tools.
  • Fiverr: Best for offering simple online services at fixed prices without managing your own website.
  • Teachable: Best for beginners who want to sell simple online courses with built-in hosting, payments, and student access.
