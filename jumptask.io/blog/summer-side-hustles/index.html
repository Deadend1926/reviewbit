Best Summer Side Hustles To Earn Extra Cash In 2026

Monika Ivanauskaite
Updated on December 27, 2025

Looking to earn some extra cash this summer without giving up your freedom? You're not alone.
As temperatures rise, so does the interest in side hustle ideas that fit around vacations, beach days, or your day job. The good news? There are plenty of low-cost, flexible options – from pet sitting to freelance work – that help you make money without the long-term commitment. Some are in person, others online, but all are worth exploring this summer.
Let’s dive in!

1. Take online surveys and complete microtasks

If you’re short on time but still want to make some extra money this season, explore microtasks and online surveys. These side hustles include things like labeling images, clicking through websites, watching ads, or answering survey questions – all things you can do from your phone. It’s not a get-rich-quick plan, but it’s an easy way to earn cash during short breaks or while binge-watching Netflix.
Platforms like JumpTask let you complete microtasks and surveys from anywhere. This works especially well in the summer, when routines are looser and flexible hours matter most. It won't replace a paycheck, but it adds up, and you can cash out when you need it.

Keep it light, keep it earning

Summer’s for easy wins. Knock out fun microtasks on JumpTask whenever you’ve got a spare minute. It’s flexible, phone-friendly, and actually pays.

2. Deliver food or groceries

Food delivery is a classic side gig, especially during the busy summer months. If you have a bike, scooter, or car, food delivery apps like Uber Eats let you work around your schedule. You can deliver takeout, groceries, or snacks to homes and parks.
Most platforms let you work on your own schedule, making it ideal for students, part-timers, or anyone juggling other commitments. The best time to drive? Dinner rush, weekends, and lunch hours – when there’s high demand and more chances for tips.
Depending on location, tips, and efficiency, you can earn anywhere from $15 to $30 per hour. It’s not just for city folks either – even small towns see a spike in delivery orders during the summer season. With minimal costs, it’s one of the fastest ways to earn extra cash without clocking into a traditional day job.

3. Sell handmade or digital products

If you enjoy crafting or creating online content, selling digital products can be a rewarding side hustle. Platforms like Etsy or Gumroad make it easy to set up shop with minimal effort. Many creators use them to sell products like planners, templates, or printables that fit seasonal trends.
Think summer-friendly items like party invites, travel checklists, or themed artwork. You can also design visuals or branding kits for small business owners and entrepreneurs.
Tools like Canva and Printify let you create and ship without much tech know-how or upfront costs. This kind of passion project is perfect for anyone looking to earn extra cash at their own pace. Even if you only sell a few items per week, you’re building a flexible stream of money from something you enjoy.

4. Offer lawn care or outdoor cleaning services

Lawn care is one of the most reliable ways for teens and high schoolers to earn extra cash during the summer. Mowing, weeding, cleaning out local pools, or even power washing patios can quickly become a steady side hustle with repeat clients.
Services like mowing lawns, leaf blowing, or sidewalk edging are easy to learn and are always needed in warm weather. You can even bundle a few options together to offer better value and increase your per-hour rate.
Promote your services in local Facebook groups or drop flyers in your neighborhood. Many homeowners are happy to outsource these tasks, especially when they’re short on time. With low start-up costs and minimal equipment, this is a great way to earn while enjoying the outdoors.

5. Pet sitting or dog walking

Pet sitting and dog walking are perfect side hustle options for animal lovers with flexible schedules during the summer. Many pet owners travel during the season, creating a demand for reliable care, from quick walks to overnight stays.
You can get paid to walk pets through apps like Rover or find local clients using neighborhood boards, flyers, or pet-friendly Facebook groups. These gigs often become loyal clients if you show reliability, care, and genuine affection for their furry friends.
Basic responsibilities include feeding, walking, and check-ins. It's a pleasing way to spend time outdoors while earning a steady income. Many families also prefer hiring someone local, especially when they know their pets are cared for by a trusted face.

6. Rent out your gear or space

Do you have a spare bike, extra camping gear, or even a room no one’s using? Renting them out can be a smart side hustle during summer, especially as more people travel or spend time outdoors. Items like beach chairs, paddleboards, or tents are in high demand during warm months.
Use platforms like Airbnb for spare rooms, or try Fat Llama and Facebook Marketplace for various gear and items. Just make sure to include clear descriptions, rental rules, and safety notes. Many platforms offer built-in insurance, but it’s always good to double-check.
This kind of gig work has very little time commitment. Once your listing is live, it can generate passive money in the background. If you manage it well, you can earn more without sacrificing your own schedule – a real win-win for side hustlers who like to keep things flexible.

7. Become a virtual assistant

A virtual assistant helps busy professionals manage their day, all from their laptop. If you’re organized and have solid writing skills, this side hustle can bring in extra money while giving you real-world experience in admin work.
Tasks may include:
  • Inbox and calendar management
  • Market research or data entry
  • Social media scheduling
  • Booking appointments or travel
You can become a virtual assistant by joining freelancer platforms like Upwork or even reaching out to local businesses through Facebook groups. Tools like Trello, Notion, or Google Calendar help keep tasks on track, especially if you’re juggling other commitments.
The best part? You set your own rates and hours. As you gain more clients, you can grow this into a great side hustle that works all year, not just during the summer.

8. Teach a skill or hobby online

Summer is the perfect time to put your talents into work. If you're good at something – playing guitar, teaching yoga, or speaking another language – you can earn extra cash by offering lessons online. With platforms like Skillshare and Teachable, it’s easy to set up your own mini classroom or sell shor, pre-recorded classes.
For beginners, 1:1 video lessons via Zoom work well. You can teach travel-friendly skills like basic Spanish or offer coaching in seasonal activities like swim techniques or fitness routines. These services are in high demand, especially among parents looking for structured options for their kids during summer.
Pair your hobby with high-income skills like communication or content creation, and you'll open doors to even more clients and steady extra income.

9. Do freelance writing or editing

If you like the art of words, freelance work like blog writing, editing, or proofreading is one of the best ways to earn extra income this summer. Brands, startups, and content creators need help with everything from product descriptions to email newsletters.
Popular platforms like Contena and WriterAccess help connect people with those looking for the service based on experience and niche. You can also reach out directly to business owners offering to help write web content or polish marketing copy.
Start simple:
  • Focus on one content type, like blog posts or newsletters
  • Build samples based on topics you enjoy
  • Use templates and free tools like Grammarly for editing help
Many side hustlers get paid to write by working with companies that need quick-turnaround content. If you’re consistent, this can lead to loyal clients and a great side hustle all year round.

10. Seasonal photography gigs and on-demand services

Photography is a fun way to earn money during the summer, especially if you like to work independently. From graduation shoots to vacation portraits and summer events, these gigs offer flexible hours and strong per-hour potential.
You don’t need expensive gear to start. Many use smartphones with editing apps like Snapseed or Canva. Offer mini sessions, family portraits, or candid in-person travel shoots, and advertise on Pinterest or local groups.
Try these tips:
  • Post sample shots on social media to reach potential clients
  • Bundle sessions with prints or digital albums
  • Offer referral discounts to earn more money with loyal clients
This is perfect if you like to work alone, and it’s easy to market through Pinterest boards for poses, outfit inspo, or seasonal backdrops.

11. Resell thrifted or clearance items

Reselling second-hand finds is a great summer side hustle that can turn a quick profit with little upfront effort. Many apps to sell stuff on, like eBay, Vinted, or Poshmark, make it easy to list items straight from your phone.
Look for deals at thrift stores, outlet malls, or garage sales and focus on seasonal goods. Items like used clothes, beachwear, sunglasses, and sandals tend to move quickly in summer.
Tips to boost sales:
  • Take bright, clear photos from multiple angles
  • Use specific keywords in your listings
  • Price competitively while leaving wiggle room for offers
It’s an easy way to earn extra money and works with any skill level. You can do it during extra hours on weekends or while traveling, just keep your listings updated and shipping options clear.

12. Participate in user testing or feedback gigs

Testing websites, apps, or digital products is a fun way to earn extra cash during your downtime. Platforms like UserTesting and Trymata connect you with companies seeking honest feedback. You simply test a product or site and share your thoughts out loud.
Most sessions take 15–30 minutes and don’t require any special background or tech knowledge. Many paid research studies follow this same format, making it accessible for just about anyone.
Here’s how to do it well:
  • Speak clearly and describe what you’re thinking as you navigate
  • Follow all instructions carefully
  • Test on both desktop and mobile if possible
There’s huge demand for everyday users, so this side hustle fits perfectly into a flexible hours lifestyle. It’s also a great way to practice communication skills while helping improve digital tools.

FAQs

What are the easiest side hustles for summer?

Some of the easiest options include pet sitting, online tutoring, garage sales, or completing microtasks from your phone. These require minimal setup and work with flexible hours, making them ideal for beginners or anyone with other summer plans.

Can I do side hustles during summer with no experience?

Yes! Many side hustle ideas like lawn care, user testing, or running errands for neighbors don’t require past experience. You just need to be responsible, communicate clearly, and start with simple services to build trust and get as many clients.

Can I earn good money with summer side gigs?

Absolutely. While not all options offer full-time pay, roles like freelance work, photography, or personal trainer sessions can bring in solid extra income. Focus on high-demand services and price your rates fairly to maximize earnings.

Which summer side hustles are best for students or part-timers?

Students often do well with gig work like tutoring, Uber Eats, or selling digital products. These fit around class schedules and offer more flexibility. Try camp counselor roles, fitness instructor, or helping out at local pools or a local farmer's market for in-person work.
