Get Paid to Give Advice: 5 Legit Ways

If you’ve ever helped someone solve a problem or offered advice that made a difference in their life, you already have what it takes to get paid to give advice. 
From relationship tips and career mentoring to life coaching that inspires personal growth, people all over the world are looking for genuine guidance and practical support. 
If you are wondering how to give advice online and get paid, this guide will show you where to start and how to turn your knowledge and skills into profit.

1. Use online platforms that connect experts with people seeking help

Money-giving advice online platforms act as a bridge between people who need advice and those who can provide it. These sites make it easy to get paid to answer questions and share your insights with people who can benefit from your guidance.
For example, someone might ask for help with a career decision, relationship issue, or business idea, and you get paid for sharing your perspective through chat, video calls, or written responses. 
To get paid to give advice, simply sign up for free, create a short profile about your skills or experience, and start connecting with users who can find value in your knowledge. 
How much money can you earn to give advice, and how much effort does it take?
Many advisors make anywhere from $20 to over $200 an hour, depending on their niche, credentials, and the value of their expertise. Some sites get you paid based on chats or session length, while others let you set your own rate.
Some of the most reputable advice-sharing platforms are:
  • Live Advice (video-based consultations): A site for one-on-one video consultations where users pay for real-time guidance and personalized feedback.
  • Skillshare (structured courses and royalties): Lets you create and upload video courses in areas like design, business, or personal development. 
  • JustAnswer (expert Q&A for pay): A Q&A platform where you get paid to answer questions in fields such as law, tech, or home improvement. 
To get started, try doing the following:
  • Join one or more expert platforms
  • Set your pricing and availability 
  • Build trust by collecting reviews and ratings
Here are three tips to help you build momentum early:
  • Start with slightly lower rates to build your profile and credibility
  • Give advice that is clear and actionable, instead of providing long explanations
  • Optimize your profile with relevant keywords so clients can easily find you

2. Build a personal brand through content

You can build your reputation by sharing valuable content online and positioning yourself as a trusted source of life advice. Start by posting practical tips and insights on a blog on your own website, YouTube channel, or podcast about topics like productivity, mental health, or personal development. You can even host video interviews with guests to offer more variety and insight. 
Then repurpose that content across social media to reach more people. As your customer base grows, you can monetize your influence through paid consultations, sponsorships, ad revenue, affiliate partnerships, digital products, or online courses.
What can you earn, and how much effort does it take to build a personal brand?
Your earnings will depend on factors like consistency, quality, and how much traffic your content attracts across platforms. You can start at zero and grow to $300–$1,500+ per month in 3–12 months with steady output. 
Established creators earn far more through multiple income streams. You can expect high upfront effort (research, scripting, editing) and consistent publishing at least once a week before you build significant momentum.
To get started, follow these five steps:
  • Find your niche and define the audience you can help and the problems you can solve.
  • Give advice on a simple blog using clear, searchable topics.
  • Create YouTube videos with practical tips, strong titles, and thumbnails.
  • Start a short podcast and repurpose episodes into clips and posts.
  • Monetize your following with paid consultations, simple digital guides, or a starter course.
Here are three tips to help you grow faster:
  • Pick one core platform and post weekly before you expand elsewhere.
  • Use a repeatable format (e.g., “3 steps + example”) so creation is quick and consistent.
  • Add clear calls to action that invite interested viewers to book a session, follow a link to your course, or join your email list.

3. Sell digital products

Selling digital products is one of the most effective ways to turn your expertise into ongoing passive income. Once you create a product, you can monetize it repeatedly with little extra work. You can focus on fitness, meal prepping, marketing, travel, self-improvement, education, or anything you have real experience in.
Digital products let you package your skills into something useful that people can download and learn from anytime.
What can you earn selling digital products, and how much effort does it take?
Earnings will depend on your niche, product quality, and marketing. Many beginners make $200–$1,000 a month, while experienced creators earn $5,000 or more from multiple products. Expect to put in solid upfront effort in planning, writing, or recording, but once your product is live, sales can continue passively with minimal maintenance.
To get started, follow these steps:
  • Choose your topic based on audience demand and a certain area of expertise that suits you best.
  • Decide on a format by creating an eBook, video course, digital guide, or templates that will allow you to get paid to write content that helps others. 
  • Set your prices wisely by starting affordably ($10–$30 for eBooks, $50–$200 for courses) and adjusting as sales grow.
  • Promote through social media, newsletters, or a simple website to reach your ideal buyers.
Some of the best sites for selling digital products include:
  • Gumroad – Great for selling eBooks, templates, and mini-courses directly to your audience.
  • Teachable – Ideal for hosting and selling online courses on different topics with video lessons and quizzes.
  • Etsy – Perfect for digital downloads like planners, guides, or printable resources.
Here are three tips to help you start earning that extra income:
  • Solve one clear problem so buyers instantly understand why your product matters.
  • Invest in simple, professional visuals such as a clean cover or thumbnail to build credibility.
  • Gather feedback early and update your product to improve quality and increase sales over time.

4. Offer coaching and consulting services

Offering coaching in general or building a consulting business lets you share your expertise directly with clients who need one-on-one guidance. This approach might work well for you if you enjoy being a personal trainer or if you are skilled in career development, business strategy, or fitness.
What can you earn offering coaching and consulting, and how much effort does it take?
Earnings can range from $50 to $300 per session for beginners. Established coaches with proven results can set  $500+ hourly rate. Coaching requires consistent effort: preparing sessions, following up with clients, and continuously improving your materials. 
To get started, follow these steps:
  • Define your target clients by identifying who benefits most from your expertise (e.g., job seekers, small business owners, or people working on fitness goals).
  • Create simple coaching plans such as one-on-one sessions, group packages, or monthly programs.
  • Set your pricing structure based on session length, depth of support, and market averages in your niche.
  • Gather testimonials after each client engagement to build social proof and trust.
Here are three tips to help you succeed as a coach or consultant:
  • Start with shorter sessions or trial offers to attract your first clients and gather feedback.
  • Be clear about outcomes and explain what clients will gain after working with you.
  • Stay consistent and visible online by posting helpful insights and success stories to attract new inquiries.

5. Test products and give your advice to developers

With product testing, you can make money or earn rewards on get-paid-to (GPT) sites for sharing your honest opinions about new apps, websites, or tools. It’s a simple way to get paid to learn about new technology while helping developers improve their products. Developers and companies rely on real users like you to point out bugs, test usability, and provide structured feedback that helps them improve their products before launch. 
What can you earn testing products, and how much effort does it take?
You can typically earn $10–$50 per test, depending on the type and length of the task. Some opportunities offer cash payments, gift cards, or crypto rewards. Most tests take only 10–30 minutes, making this one of the easiest side hustles to fit into your day.

  • Provide clear and structured feedback by describing what works well, what could be improved, and any issues you notice.
  • Stay consistent by checking for new opportunities daily and completing tasks that match your skills and interests.
