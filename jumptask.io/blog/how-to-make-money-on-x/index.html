Official X (formerly Twitter) monetization programs

In 2026, X finally feels built for creators. The platform now pays close attention to real engagement, not just follower counts. With ads revenue sharing, payouts come from views by Premium users, which means quality posts matter more than ever. 
From threads to Spaces, X is giving creators more ways to turn attention into income – and each feature comes with its own rules, perks, and earning potential.

Ads Revenue Sharing (Creator Ad Revenue Program)

The ads revenue sharing system lets creators earn a slice of revenue generated from ads shown in the replies under their posts. X uses a revenue-sharing model that rewards posts with high engagement and reach.
To join, you’ll need:
  • To be an X Premium subscriber or have a Twitter Blue badge.
  • At least 500 followers, 5M impressions over the past three months.
  • A connected Stripe account.
  • An active account in good standing.
Most payouts land at around $8.50 per million verified impressions, but that number moves. It depends on how much ad revenue X pulls in and how well your posts perform. Creators who post regularly often see small but steady payouts.
Quick tips to hit the impression threshold:
  • Post often and jump into your own replies – that’s where visibility grows fastest. 
  • Add visuals that stand out or a clean header image to grab attention. 
  • Create engaging and shareable content.
Over time, the impressions add up and unlock real earning power.

Subscriptions (formerly Super Follows)

With subscriptions, creators can charge a monthly fee to share bonus content with their most loyal audience. Think of it as X’s version of Patreon, but built right into your X (Twitter) account. 
You keep up to 97% of your earnings until you hit $50K in total payouts, then the rate adjusts to 90%, which still beats many other social media platforms
Subscribers often pay for:
  • Early access to content.
  • Exclusive posts.
  • Behind-the-scenes content.
  • Private Spaces.
  • Subscriber-only replies that make them feel part of your community. 
The key is value: post consistently, reply often, and offer moments fans can’t get elsewhere. 
Start small – even a $2 or $3 plan works while gaining followers. As your followers increase, so can your monthly price. Over time, this model rewards creators who deliver consistency and keep their X account active.

Ticketed Spaces

Ticketed Spaces are X’s version of live podcasts, but with your followers right there in the room. This feature lets you charge a small fee for entry, turning real-time chats into income. 
Eligible creators must:
  • Have at least 1,000 followers.
  • Have hosted a few Spaces in the past month.
Once that’s done, you’re ready to go.
Go live, share what you know, and answer a few quick questions. Talk about what your crowd already likes. Keep it low-key, nothing fancy. Invite your most engaged followers and just hang out for a bit. 
Tickets are handled through a third-party payment service, so setup is easy. What matters most is energy – if you keep sessions fun, people will come back and tell their friends.

Tips (Tip Jar)

The Tip Jar is the simplest way to earn on X. It lets your followers send small payments directly to you as a thank-you for valuable or engaging content. Think of it like dropping a few coins in a creator’s jar after a good performance – quick, easy, and personal.
You can connect a Stripe account or another 3rd party payment service, such as Cash App to enable tips. Make sure your X account is verified and your payment details are complete before enabling the feature.
A little gratitude goes a long way here. When someone tips you, thank them publicly. Supporters love recognition, and those shout-outs often spark more contributions from your active audience.

Alternate ways to make earn money on X

Not every creator qualifies for X’s official monetization, but that doesn’t mean you can’t make money there. Third-party methods give you a bit more say in how you price your work and connect with people who actually care about it. 

Affiliate marketing

Affiliate marketing is still one of the simplest ways to earn money on X. You share a link to a product or service, and if someone buys through it, you get a small cut. No fancy setup, just honest recommendations that fit your audience.
It works best when you’re already talking about things people want – tech tools, online courses, or even everyday digital goodies. A quick tip most creators miss: drop your link in the first comment instead of the post itself. The X algorithm doesn’t love external links, and this helps your reach.
Add a short clip or photo showing what you’re talking about. Posts that feel real perform better than polished ads. Over time, good affiliate marketing can turn your everyday posts into small, steady paydays.

Sponsored content aka brand deals

Sponsored content is exactly what it sounds like – you get paid by brands to talk about their stuff. It could be a quick review, a shout-out, or a fun post that shows the product in use. It’s been around for years on X, and it’s still one of the most reliable monetization strategies out there.
Don’t price yourself only by follower count. Brands care more about your engagement rates – how often people reply, share, or actually buy. When pitching, drop a screenshot of your analytics to show proof.
Stick with companies your niche audience already likes. Keep your posts natural, and brand-safe content will follow. The more honest you sound, the more deals come your way – and that’s how you keep the money on X steady.

Selling digital products or services

Selling your own digital products on X is another way to earn. You can share:
  • Templates you’ve built. 
  • Short courses.
  • E-books.
  • Even quick consulting calls. 
It’s less about chasing sponsored posts and more about turning a hobby that makes money into something people actually want to buy. There’s no need for a big setup, just a clear idea and a place to sell it.
Use X threads to tell the story behind your offer – what problem it solves, how you built it, and why it helps. People love a peek behind the scenes. You can link to sites like Gumroad or share Notion templates for simple checkout options.
Creators who mix storytelling with smart linking usually see steady product sales. It’s proof that sharing what you know, not just what you sell, builds trust. And trust is what turns posts into profit on X.

Building an email newsletter

Many creators use X to grow their email list and turn followers into long-term readers. Newsletters give you a direct line to your audience, far from algorithms or social media platforms that change every month. 
Tools like Substack or Beehiiv make it simple to send updates, sell digital products, or share deeper thoughts that don’t fit in a post. A free weekly roundup works great as a lead magnet – short, useful, and easy to share. 
Over time, those emails build trust and drive product sales from people who already like your work. It’s one of the smartest monetization strategies because it keeps your followers coming back while giving you full control of your income stream.

Tips on how to get started and stand out

On X, visibility makes or breaks your growth. The more people see you, the more chances you have to unlock monetization features and turn attention into income. So, here are some tips:
  • Post two to four times a day – short takes, quick stories, or something funny that fits your niche. Stay focused on one theme so your right audience knows what you’re about. 
  • Add videos, polls, or images that stand out while scrolling.
  • Before posting, tidy up your profile. Use a clear profile picture and fill in your details. A complete profile helps with trust and looks better when brands check you out for sponsored tweets or brand deals.
  • Keep your professional account safe with two-factor authentication and a verified email address. 
  • Talk to people – don’t just post and vanish. Reply, quote, and actively interact in threads. That’s how your name starts showing up more.
  • If you have an active premium subscription, use those tools to boost visibility on your best posts. Mix in older hits too. 
  • Repurpose top tweets into quick clips or threads – a little recycling goes a long way. 
So can you make money on Twitter? Absolutely! But it takes time, steady posting, real effort, and a bit of patience.
