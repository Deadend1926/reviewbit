Freecash is one of the most popular GPT (get-paid-to) platforms, where people can get paid for completing surveys, playing games, and engaging with offerwalls. But there are other options out there.
Some alternatives have faster payouts, lower withdrawal limits, or more ways to earn. Many of these GPT sites offer mobile accessibility through the Apple App Store and Google play store, allowing you to earn in your spare time.
Here are nine other websites like Freecash that can help you make money online.

1. JumpTask

  • Unique features: Instant withdrawals, no hidden fees, higher earning potential with advanced tasks.
  • Earning methods: Surveys, microtasks, playing games, advanced tasks.
  • Payout: Crypto-based payout that can be exchanged for cash via partners.
  • User reputation: Rated highly for fast transactions and an intuitive interface.
  • Why it’s better than Freecash: Offers more diverse earning options, faster payouts, and advanced tasks that can increase income potential.
JumpTask is a microtasking platform that offers multiple ways to earn rewards, including taking surveys, playing games, and completing social tasks. Unlike many other platforms, it focuses on engagement-based earnings, making it an interactive way to earn money online.
What makes JumpTask stand out is its instant payout system. Unlike Freecash, which may take time to process withdrawals, JumpTask allows users to cash out immediately with no hidden fees. It also offers more diverse earning methods beyond traditional offer walls, including training AI systems, data entry tasks – perfect for people who prefer independent, low-pressure work environments.
Most people appreciate Jumptasks’s instant payouts and diverse and engaging opportunities. However, some have suggested improvements regarding task availability or more options in advanced tasks.

Try a smarter way to earn

2. Swagbucks

  • Unique features: Shopping cashback, daily bonuses, frequent promotions.
  • Earning methods: Videos, cashback rewards, search earnings.
  • Payout: $1 minimum for gift cards, $5 for PayPal.
  • User reputation: Well-rated for its low cashout limits and variety of earning options.
  • Why it’s better than Freecash: More ways to earn, lower payout thresholds for gift cards, and extra cash through promotions and deals.
Swagbucks is one of the oldest and most well-known get-paid-to sites. People can get paid to do various things, such as answering surveys, shopping online for cashback, and even searching the web. Unlike Freecash, Swagbucks offers multiple ways to earn passively.
In the context of JumpTask vs. Swagbucks, this platform remains the preferred choice for those who value traditional retail rewards over decentralized cryptocurrency.
One of Swagbucks’ biggest advantages is its low minimum payout, with gift cards available for as little as $1. PayPal withdrawals start at $5, making it easier to withdraw out without waiting too long. People also appreciate the frequent promo codes to save money and daily bonuses. 
However, some users have reported that surveys can be time-consuming and often result in disqualifications, making it frustrating to earn consistently.

3. InboxDollars

  • Unique features: Real money payments, $5 instant sign-up bonus, scratch card prizes.
  • Earning methods: Surveys, watching videos, reading paid emails.
  • Payout: $15 minimum for PayPal and gift cards.
  • User reputation: Liked for its straightforward earning structure but noted for its higher cashout limit.
  • Why it’s better than Freecash: Pays in real money with no points conversion, and people can earn extra cash with paid emails and scratch prizes.
InboxDollars is a popular GPT site where you don’t earn points, but get real money instead of it. Unlike Freecash, where people earn virtual currency that needs to be converted, InboxDollars pays directly in dollars, making it easier to track income.
People can get paid to take as many surveys as they want, read paid emails, watch videos, and even play scratch card games to earn cash in their spare time. While the minimum payout of $15 is higher than some alternatives, InboxDollars offers a $5 sign-up bonus to help new users start earning right away. This is an advantage over Freecash, which does not provide a free sign-up incentive.

4. PrizeRebel

  • Unique features: Instant withdrawals for high-tier users, bonus awards for loyal users.
  • Earning methods: Surveys, offerwalls, watch ads, microtasks.
  • Payout: $2 minimum for Amazon gift cards, $5 for PayPal.
  • User reputation: Positive feedback for quick payouts and loyalty perks.
  • Why it’s better than Freecash: Faster cashouts for frequent users and lower minimum payout thresholds.
PrizeRebel is a GPT app like Freecash, known for its quick payout processing and rewards system that benefits active users. It provides multiple ways to earn, including surveys, microtasks, and video ads, with lower cashout limits compared to Freecash.
One of the key perks of PrizeRebel is its instant withdrawals for VIP users. The more active you are, the faster your cashouts become. Amazon gift cards start at just $2, while PayPal cashouts begin at $5, making it one of the lower payout thresholds among GPT sites.

5. Prolific

  • Unique features: High-paying surveys, fewer disqualifications.
  • Earning methods: Academic research surveys.
  • Payout: $5 minimum via PayPal.
  • User reputation: Rated highly for fair pay and consistent survey availability.
  • Why it’s better than Freecash: Higher payouts per survey and guaranteed eligibility for most studies.
Prolific is a survey rewards platform focused on academic research, which means people typically earn higher payouts than on Freecash. The platform is known for its low disqualification rate, meaning people don’t waste time starting surveys they can’t complete.
Earnings on Prolific are significantly better than most GPT sites, averaging $6–$10 per hour, compared to market research surveys that may pay only a few cents per response. The minimum payout is $5 via PayPal, making it a solid choice for those looking for consistent and well-paid survey work.

6. Respondent

  • Unique features: High-paying research studies, professional focus groups.
  • Earning methods: Online studies, one-on-one interviews, focus groups.
  • Payout: PayPal payments with a $5 minimum.
  • User reputation: Well-rated for high payouts but selective screening process.
  • Why it’s better than Freecash: Higher earnings per study, fewer low-paying and research-based opportunities.
Respondent is a premium research platform where people get paid to participate in online studies and focus groups. Unlike Freecash, which mainly offers simple tasks and to take surveys, Respondent provides opportunities to earn $50 or more per study.
Since Respondent focuses on research, qualifying for studies can take time. However, people can earn significantly more once accepted than on traditional GPT sites. Payments are processed via PayPal, and most studies take 30–60 minutes to complete.

7. Field Agent

  • Unique features: Real-world jobs, no survey disqualifications.
  • Earning methods: In-store assignments, product audits, and mystery shopping.
  • Payout: Direct bank deposit or PayPal, no high minimum threshold.
  • User reputation: Praised for decent pay and unique opportunities, but some users report limited tasks in certain areas.
  • Why it’s better than Freecash: Higher earnings per task, real-world work opportunities, and no risk of disqualification.
Field Agent is a task-based rewards platform that pays people to complete real-world assignments, such as checking store displays, taking product photos, and auditing in-store promotions. Unlike traditional GPT sites, it offers opportunities to earn money outside of surveys and other tasks online.
Users typically earn between $5–$15 per assignment, depending on the complexity of the task. Payments are made via bank transfer or PayPal, ensuring flexibility. Reviews highlight the app’s reliability and decent payouts, though some users mention that task availability varies by location.

8. Idle-Empire

  • Unique features: Low withdrawal limit, gaming rewards, multiple crypto options.
  • Earning methods: Surveys, offers, playing games, and other tasks online.
  • Payout: As low as $0.10 for crypto rewards (Bitcoin, Ethereum, Litecoin, etc.).
  • User reputation: Positive feedback for fast payouts and crypto rewards, but some report occasional lulls in available jobs.
  • Why it’s better than Freecash: One of the lowest cashout thresholds in the industry, pays users in popular cryptocurrencies, and offers a 500-point sign-up bonus to help users cash out faster.
Idle-Empire offers various ways to earn, including completing surveys, offers, and participating in playing games. People can earn points through these activities, which can be exchanged for rewards such as gift cards or cryptocurrency. One standout feature is its extremely low withdrawal limit, starting as low as $0.10 for crypto rewards, making it easy for users to cash out quickly.
User reviews highlight the platform’s user-friendly interface and quick payouts, especially for cryptocurrency users. However, some people suggest that the number of available surveys and offers can be inconsistent, depending on location.

9. Qmee

  • Unique features: Instant PayPal withdrawals, no minimum cashout, streak bonuses.
  • Earning methods: Surveys, shopping cashback, and web searches.
  • Payout: Instant PayPal withdrawals, no cashout threshold.
  • User reputation: Positive feedback for easy money and instant payments, but some people feel the surveys could be more frequent.
  • Why it’s better than Freecash: Instant PayPal cash withdrawals, money from searches and shopping deals, and extra money for consistent activity.
Qmee is a rewards platform that lets people earn money by answering surveys and earning shopping online cashback, and they can even get paid to do web searches. The platform stands out with its no minimum payout feature, allowing users to withdraw earnings instantly via PayPal, without having to reach a specific cashout threshold.
Reviews highlight how convenient it is to earn while browsing the web, and many people appreciate instant PayPal withdrawals. However, some note that survey availability can sometimes be limited, and the earnings per survey aren’t always as high as they’d like.

Comparing top websites like Freecash that pay real money

All of these GPT sites are reliable and provide various ways to earn extra cash online. While they won’t make you rich, they can be a solid side hustle. It’s a good idea to try a few and find out which ones work best for you, as survey and offer availability can differ based on your location and profile.
