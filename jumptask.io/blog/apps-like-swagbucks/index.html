2. InboxDollars

InboxDollars is a popular online survey site for people who want extra cash without fuss. You get paid for taking surveys, doing polls, and sometimes playing games in your free time. 
The site is tied to Swagbucks, so it runs in a similar way. Don’t expect huge wins, but a few survey tasks can bring in a few dollars here and there.
  • Earning potential: from $5 to $100 per month.
  • Payout: PayPal cash, checks, and gift cards.
  • What users like: wide range of tasks, unique email offers, easy cash rewards.
  • What users don’t like: low payouts on some tasks, US-only focus.
  • Best for: US-based users seeking a familiar, all-in-one rewards platform with varied earning options.

3. Freecash

Freecash is a rewards site for quick wins when you want free cash fast. You earn by completing simple tasks, like playing games or trying offers, so it feels different from online surveys. 
Many people use it as one of the sites like Swagbucks but better because payouts can come quicker and land as real money, including options like prepaid Visa cards. 
  • Earning potential: from $5 to $150 per month.
  • Payout: PayPal cash, cryptocurrencies (Bitcoin, Ethereum, Litecoin), and gift cards.
  • What users like: high-paying offers, fast payouts (can get paid instantly), and global availability.
  • What users don’t like: occasional offer tracking issues, some geographic restrictions.
  • Best for: users seeking a modern, fast-paying platform with a wide range of tasks and multiple payout options, including crypto.
It’s not a magic money button, but it can bring in extra money online during your free time.

4. Survey Junkie

Survey Junkie is a safe pick if you want a straight paid survey setup. It’s one of the better other survey apps and feels simpler than most sites like Swagbucks. 
You just start taking surveys, stack earn points, and trade them for cash rewards. Cash-outs go to your bank account, so it’s easy to track what you earn money from. It won’t be huge, but it'll steadily earn cash for spare minutes.
  • Earning potential: from $5 to $100 per month.
  • Payout: cash via PayPal and gift cards.
  • What users like: simple, user-friendly interface of Survey Junkie, steady flow of surveys, reliable payouts.
  • What users don’t like: frequent survey disqualifications, limited to survey-only tasks.
  • Best for: those who prefer a focused, no-frills survey platform with competitive payouts.
Survey Junkie focuses on consumer and market research surveys, making it a simple choice for anyone who prefers one task type to earn extra cash.

5. Clickworker

Clickworker is one of the other apps similar to Swagbucks that feels more like real task work. You can earn money online through short jobs, not just completing online surveys. 
If you’ve tried MTurk, Clickworker feels similar but simpler to start.
Try small gigs when you want a break from many survey apps. It’s flexible, so the earning pace is up to you.
  • Earning potential: from $10 to $200 per month.
  • Payout: cash via PayPal and bank transfer.
  • What users like: diverse tasks, flexible schedule, global access.
  • What users don’t like: irregular task availability, low pay on simpler tasks.
  • Best for: those wanting flexible, part-time microtask work to supplement their income.

6. PrizeRebel

PrizeRebel is a rewards site for people who want more than surveys. You can mix taking surveys, quick videos, and light game apps in your free time. 
Points stack as earn points and bonus points. Then you trade them for cash or free gift cards. It’s simple, and the steady extra cash online can add up.
  • Earning potential: from $5 to $100 per month.
  • Payout: PayPal, gift cards, and crypto via Coinbase.
  • What users like: solid referral system, daily bonuses, steady promotions, and diverse tasks.
  • What users don’t like: some tasks offer low pay for time spent; careful selection is key.
  • Best for: users who enjoy a flexible platform with multiple earning methods and want to explore both cash and crypto rewards.

7. LifePoints

LifePoints is a paid survey option among sites like Swagbucks. The free app pays you for taking surveys and polls. Cash out your earned rewards to a bank account.
  • Earning potential: from $5 to $100 per month.
  • Payout: PayPal money and gift cards.
  • What users like: global accessibility, user-friendly design, regular survey availability.
  • What users don’t like: occasional disqualifications, limited task types beyond surveys.
  • Best for: survey lovers worldwide who want a straightforward way to earn with their opinions.

8. Qmee

Qmee is a simple rewards site if you want quick payouts with no minimum. You earn cash from short survey tasks, plus polls when they pop up. 
It also lets you shop online for cashback, so small buys can turn into pocket money. Expect light rewards, not big money.
  • Earning potential: from $5 to $100 per month.
  • Payout: PayPal money, gift cards, or charity donations.
  • What users like: no cash-out minimum, instant PayPal payouts, browser extension for cashback and paid searches.
  • What users don’t like: limited earning opportunities outside surveys, some regions have fewer tasks.
  • Best for: users who want quick, flexible cashouts and a clean, survey-focused experience.

9. Toluna

Toluna is a nice fit if you want your opinions to actually shape products. It’s one of the paid survey picks in applications like Swagbucks, with steady survey opportunities and plenty of fun online activities. You earn points for taking surveys, then trade them rewards or small extra money.
  • Earning potential: from $5 to $50 per month.
  • Payout: PayPal or gift cards.
  • What users like: fun community features, interactive polls, product testing, and consistent survey availability.
  • What users don’t like: points can take time to accumulate, and reward redemption thresholds may feel high.
  • Best for: users who enjoy a more social survey experience and don’t mind taking a slower, steady route to rewards.

10. ySense

ySense is a rewards site with lots of survey opportunities and quick tasks. You can make money online by completing surveys and small jobs in minutes. 
Points stack as you go, then you trade them to earn rewards. Its worldwide access beats many other survey apps that stay in the U.S. only.
  • Earning potential: from $5 to $100 per month.
  • Payout: PayPal, gift cards, and Payoneer.
  • What users like: wide variety of tasks, strong referral program, and global accessibility.
  • What users don’t like: occasional low-paying tasks and some geographic restrictions on higher-paying offers.
  • Best for: international users seeking a flexible platform with a good variety of surveys, tasks, and offers.

11. Pinecone Research

Pinecone Research is one of the many survey apps where you can earn money in a simple way. It feels more selective than most survey apps, and you often get better paid survey invites sent straight to you. 
You won’t find playing games, big online shopping perks, or shop online bonuses here. Instead, it sticks to solid market research and occasional product tests. 
If you want steady earn points that turn into earn free cash, this free website keeps things focused.
  • Earning potential: typically $3 to $5 per completed survey, adding up to $20–$100 per month depending on activity.
  • Payout: PayPal cash, gift cards, and bank transfers.
  • What users like: consistently high survey payouts, straightforward point system, occasional free product trials.
  • What users don’t like: limited survey availability, strict membership acceptance, and invite-only access in some regions.
  • Best for: individuals looking for premium surveys with reliable, above-average compensation and occasional product testing perks.

12. Branded Surveys

Branded Surveys is one of the best apps for answering surveys and making money online. It runs loyalty bonuses, so users pick up more money over time. 
If you like to earn points that turn into free money, this website is the way to go.
  • Earning potential: from $5 to $100+ per month.
  • Payout: PayPal cash, free gift cards, and direct bank transfer (U.S. only).
  • What users like: fast payouts, daily poll bonuses and simple dashboard.
  • What users don’t like: occasional survey disqualifications and fewer offers for non-U.S. users.
  • Best for: users who prefer survey-focused platforms with consistent earnings and a straightforward payout process.
