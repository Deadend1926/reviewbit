1. ReviewBit

ReviewBit is a leading GPT (get-paid-to) platform where users can earn extra cash by completing simple and fun microtasks online. You can take surveys, play games, try new apps, and even make money by watching ads.
ReviewBit stands out with its instant crypto payouts – there’s no minimum threshold, so you can withdraw as soon as you finish a task.
  • Earning potential: From $5 to $100 per month.
  • Payout: $JMPT and cryptocurrency.
  • What users like: instant crypto withdrawals, a wide range of fun task types, and a user-friendly interface.
  • What users don’t like: task volume can vary.
  • Best for: beginners who want fast payouts, flexible gigs, and an easy way to earn in spare time.
The platform is easy to navigate, making it perfect for beginners who want to earn online in their spare time. Try a few task types first to see what fits you best. Small wins add up fast. If you’re looking for a fun and flexible way to make money online, ReviewBit is a great place to start.

More task variety, fewer limits

2. Amazon Mechanical Turk

Amazon Mechanical Turk (MTurk) is a long-running crowdsourcing platform where you get paid to complete small gigs online. Think image tagging, surveys, and content moderation.
What makes MTurk different is who uses it. A lot of the work comes from researchers, developers, and companies that need real human input. It also supports academic studies, AI training, and human verification gigs, which helps keep gigs coming in more consistently.
  • Earning potential: From $5 to $80 per month.
  • Payout: Direct bank transfer (U.S. only) or Amazon gift cards (non-U.S. users).
  • What users like: it’s legitimate and well-established, there’s a steady stream of gigs, and you can choose from a wide range of gigs.
  • What users don’t like: plenty of gigs pay low, so you have to filter to find the better ones, and cash payouts are limited to U.S. users.
  • Best for: people who want consistent job availability and don’t mind sorting to find worthwhile gigs.
One tip: sort tasks by reward and time first. Save good requesters once you find them. That small habit can raise your hourly rate without doing more work.

3. Appen

Appen is similar to Clickworker because you complete paid online microtasks. Most jobs focus on training AI systems. Common gigs include data annotation, transcription, and search result evaluation.
Appen stands out with project-based work that can last weeks. Many projects offer fixed hourly pay and training. You often need to pass a qualification test first. Once approved, the work can feel steadier than per-task platforms.
  • Earning potential: Around $50 to $400+ per month, depending on projects.
  • Payout: Monthly via Payoneer or direct deposit, based on location.
  • What users like: reliable payments, higher hourly rates for qualified workers, and longer projects.
  • What users don’t like: gaps between projects, inconsistent task availability, and qualification steps.
  • Best for: people who want hourly pay and longer-term gigs.
To get more invites, finish onboarding carefully and keep your accuracy high. It also helps to check your dashboard daily. New jobs can appear fast and fill up quickly.

4. Toloka

Toloka is a microtask platform owned by Yandex that offers small, easy online jobs almost anyone can do. Gigs include image categorization, voice recording, text proofreading, plus simple data collection and light data entry gigs that only need basic skills.
Toloka stands out with a super low minimum withdrawal and a mobile app. That makes it feel more casual than many other websites. 
  • Earning potential: Around $5 to $60 per month, depending on task access.
  • Payout: Cashout starts at $0.02 via PayPal, Payoneer, or Skrill.
  • What users like: fast withdrawals, very low payout minimum, and mobile-friendly earning.
  • What users don’t like: you need to complete many small gigs to make money, and task quality can vary by region.
  • Best for: people who want quick, casual gigs with easy cashouts.
You can earn extra money in your free time, knock out a few gigs, and cash out fast. Some gigs also involve testing apps or quick checks for digital products, depending on your region.

5. Remotasks

Remotasks is one of microtask websites that specializes in AI training projects. It offers detailed microtasks like image annotation, 3D LiDAR labeling, and content categorization.
Remotasks stands out because you can level up. It offers free training courses that unlock higher-paying tasks. Over time, this can lead to better rates than basic survey-style platforms. Weekly PayPal payouts also help, since there’s no minimum threshold.
  • Earning potential: Around $30 to $500+ per month, depending on skill level.
  • Payout: Weekly PayPal payments, no minimum threshold.
  • What users like: steady task flow for active users, fair pay on advanced gigs, and free training that boosts earnings.
  • What users don’t like: it can take effort to reach top-paying gigs, and getting started may feel slow.
  • Best for: people willing to learn for higher-paying microtasks.
If you want to make money online fast, read all the details and follow the training. It’s not about games or answering surveys. It’s a platform with paid jobs and services for workers.

6. Microworkers

Microworkers is a global crowdsourcing marketplace where users get money for completing small online jobs like surveys, testing, and content moderation. 
Microworkers stands out for its international access and wide task variety. You’ll also see more marketing-style gigs than on many platforms, like social media engagement, app testing, and occasional video tagging. Signup is quick, but verification can take a bit.
  • Earning potential: From $10 to $120 per month, depending on gis available.
  • Payout: Minimum $9 via PayPal, Skrill, and other supported methods.
  • What users like: lots of task options, global availability, and a mix of testing and promo gigs.
  • What users don’t like: account verification can take time, and some gigs pay low.
  • Best for: users who want worldwide access and a broader mix of small gigs.
If you’re mainly answering surveys, earnings can stay small. Mix in testing and promo jobs for higher pay. 
This platform works with many websites, so workers can pick paid gigs that turn spare time into cash.

7. Prolific

If you use Clickworker for surveys, Prolific will feel familiar. It’s built around academic studies, so the surveys tend to be better written and pay more fairly for your time. 
You also waste less time on screening compared to many survey websites. You can withdraw once you hit £6 (around $6), and payouts go to PayPal.
  • Earning potential: Around $20 to $150 per month, depending on study availability.
  • Payout: Minimum £6 (~$6) via PayPal.
  • What users like: clear instructions and study details, fair rewards for the time spent, and fast payouts.
  • What users don’t like: survey availability can be limited, and spots can fill quickly.
  • Best for: anyone tired of low-paying surveys and constant disqualifications.
If your goal is to make money without jumping between sketchy websites, Prolific feels safer than many websites. It’s not about games or random offers. It’s a research platform that pays cash, so the process stays simple.
However, if you still want fast cashout, but not the academic vibe, consider Freecash.

8. UserTesting

On UserTesting, you try out websites and applications and say what you’re thinking. Your screen records the session, along with your voice, so the company hears your real-time reactions.
One test is often 10–20 minutes and pays $10. That beats grinding tiny gigs for cents. If you qualify for tests often, it can turn into a nice monthly extra. 
The annoying part is access. You won’t get invited every day, and some tests are picky.
  • Earning potential: Around $20 to $300+ per month, depending on invites.
  • Payout: $10 per test via PayPal within 7 days.
  • What users like: strong pay per test, straightforward gigs, and fast PayPal payouts.
  • What users don’t like: not everyone qualifies, and tests aren’t always available.
  • Best for: people who like giving feedback out loud and want better pay.
If you want to make money without endless gigs, this platform can feel refreshing. You’re doing paid feedback for real websites and digital services. It’s not games, but the money per test is solid when invites show up.

FAQs

What’s better than Clickworker really depends on how you like to work. Some other websites offer more task variety or a lower minimum payout. If you prefer data collection, image labeling, or testing websites, a few platforms can feel like a smarter way to get paid.

There isn’t one No. 1 money app for everyone. Some applications are better for answering surveys or playing games, while others focus on data entry or testing work. The best option depends on your free time, payout speed, and how easily you want to get money.

The best one for side hustles is the one you’ll actually use. Applications that let you get paid for completing surveys, simple data jobs, or quick tests work well around busy schedules. If you want higher pay, look for platforms offering higher paying tasks instead of basic gigs.
